Pawel Tulin

Lord Abbett — Enterprise Investment Platform & Digital Ecosystem

VP of UX · Information Architecture · Product Design · UX Research

Context

Lord Abbett is a global investment management firm serving individual investors, financial advisors, and large institutional clients. The firm manages significant assets and operates in a highly regulated, security-critical financial environment where data accuracy, transaction integrity, and user trust are paramount.

Lord Abbett engaged a specialized digital agency (HNW – High Net Worth) to redesign and rebuild its entire digital ecosystem. I initially joined the project as an Information Architect and UX Designer, and was later promoted to VP of UX, assuming full responsibility for experience strategy, delivery, and team leadership.

The project spanned more than two years and was one of the most data-intensive financial platforms I have worked on.

Challenge

The existing digital presence could not support the complexity of Lord Abbett's business:

  • Multiple distinct user groups with fundamentally different needs
  • Massive volumes of financial and market data from internal and external sources
  • Legacy backend systems that could not be replaced
  • Strict security, compliance, and transaction-integrity requirements
  • Highly complex investment and transaction workflows
  • A large, dynamically generated website with thousands of active pages

The challenge was to design a single coherent system that could serve all users safely, efficiently, and at scale.

Users

Through extensive research, we identified three primary front-end user groups:

  • Individual investors
  • Financial advisors
  • Institutional investors

Each group differed significantly in:

  • Decision-making behavior
  • Data consumption patterns
  • Risk tolerance
  • Frequency and complexity of transactions

On the backend, we also designed for:

  • Data analysts
  • System administrators
  • External vendors and partners
Research & Discovery

We began with deep discovery work:

  • Stakeholder interviews across business, technology, and operations
  • User interviews across all client segments
  • Journey mapping and task analysis
  • Competitive and comparative analysis

This work surfaced major gaps in:

  • Information architecture
  • Navigation and content discovery
  • Data comprehension and visualization
  • Transaction safety and error prevention
My Role Expansion

Mid-project, I was promoted to VP of UX, with responsibility for:

  • UX strategy and vision
  • Team hiring and leadership
  • Project scheduling and delivery
  • P&L ownership
  • Client and account management
  • Cross-functional alignment

This transformed my role from contributor to executive owner of the experience.

Information Architecture & Navigation

The platform consisted of thousands of dynamically generated pages, making IA a critical foundation.

I led:

  • A complete restructuring of the information architecture
  • User-group-specific navigation systems
  • Context-aware content models

Each user group experienced a tailored structure optimized for how they consumed data and executed tasks.

Data Visualization & Insight

One of the core challenges was presenting large volumes of financial data in a usable way.

We developed:

  • A custom visual language for financial data
  • Dashboards tailored to each user group
  • Drill-down capabilities from high-level overviews to granular details

The goal was to allow users to:

  • Understand performance quickly
  • Explore trends and anomalies
  • Take confident, informed action
Transaction Design & Security

Because large sums of money moved through the system, there was zero tolerance for error.

I led the design of:

  • Transaction flows with multiple checks and balances
  • Fallback and recovery mechanisms
  • Clear error prevention and messaging patterns
  • Role-based permissions and safeguards

These flows were prototyped, tested, and refined repeatedly.

Prototyping & Usability Testing

We modeled and prototyped hundreds of transaction scenarios.

To validate them:

  • We partnered with an external usability testing firm
  • Created detailed testing scripts and scenarios
  • Tested across all user groups
  • Iterated continuously until flows met both usability and compliance standards
Legacy Systems & Integrations

The platform had to integrate with:

  • Multiple legacy internal systems
  • External data providers
  • Banking partners and financial institutions

While backend systems could not be changed, we designed frontend experiences that:

  • Abstracted complexity
  • Presented consistent interaction patterns
  • Reduced cognitive and operational risk
Mobile Applications

Insights gained during the project led to several mobile initiatives:

  • Advisor apps for sales and client presentations
  • Investor apps for monitoring portfolios and executing transactions
  • Institutional tools for rapid market response

Each app addressed a specific use case while sharing a common design system foundation.

Design System & CMS

I designed a component system library — effectively an early design system — built on top of a custom CMS developed specifically for Lord Abbett.

  • Design rules were encoded directly into the CMS
  • New pages automatically adhered to system standards
  • Consistency was enforced without slowing content creation

This approach ensured long-term scalability and governance.

Outcomes
  • A unified digital ecosystem supporting all user groups
  • Safer, clearer transaction workflows
  • Improved data comprehension and decision-making
  • Strong client and stakeholder satisfaction
  • A scalable foundation for future digital growth

The project delivered measurable business value while significantly modernizing Lord Abbett's digital capabilities.
