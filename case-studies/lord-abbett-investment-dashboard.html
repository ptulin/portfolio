Pawel Tulin

Lord Abbett — Digital Ecosystem Transformation

VP of UX · Product Design · UX Research · Information Architecture

Overview

Lord Abbett is a global asset management firm serving individual investors, financial advisors, and institutional clients. The company engaged HNW (High Net Worth Digital Products) to redesign and rebuild its entire digital ecosystem — spanning public websites, transactional platforms, internal tools, and mobile applications.

This was a multi-year, mission-critical transformation involving massive data volumes, complex financial workflows, strict security requirements, and a highly diverse user base. Dashboards were only one component of a much larger system.

I joined the project initially as an Information Architect and UX Designer and was later promoted to VP of UX, taking full ownership of UX strategy, delivery, team leadership, and account responsibility.

Scope & Responsibility
  • UX strategy and information architecture for a large-scale financial platform
  • End-to-end product and experience design across web and mobile
  • Executive ownership of UX delivery, scheduling, staffing, and P&L
  • Hiring and leading multidisciplinary design teams
  • Client leadership and account management
  • Cross-functional coordination with engineering, data teams, vendors, and external banks

The project ran for over two years and involved continuous discovery, design, prototyping, testing, and iteration.

Users & Complexity

Through extensive qualitative research, interviews, and field studies, we identified multiple primary and secondary user groups:

Primary users:

  • Individual investors
  • Financial advisors
  • Institutional investors

Secondary and backend users:

  • Data analysts
  • System administrators
  • Internal operations teams
  • External data vendors
  • Partner banks and financial institutions

Each group had fundamentally different goals, data needs, decision-making patterns, risk tolerance, and interaction models. Designing a single system that could serve all of them required deep personalization, role-based experiences, and rigorous access control.

Research & Discovery

We conducted extensive upfront and ongoing research, including:

  • In-depth interviews with all major user groups
  • Journey mapping across investment, research, and transaction workflows
  • Shadowing and observation of real-world financial decision processes
  • Analysis of legacy systems, data sources, and operational constraints

The platform handled enormous volumes of data from both internal and external sources, making this one of the most data-heavy products I've worked on.

Research insights directly informed information architecture, navigation models, data visualization strategies, and transaction flow design.

Information Architecture & Navigation

The website and platform were extremely large — with thousands of dynamically generated pages at any given time.

Key challenges:

  • Preventing users from getting lost in complex data structures
  • Ensuring fast access to relevant information
  • Supporting very different mental models across user types

I designed a new information architecture and multiple navigation systems tailored to each primary user group. IA decisions became the foundation for the entire platform and were critical to its scalability and usability.

Product & UX Design

Data Visualization

One of the most difficult challenges was presenting large volumes of financial data in a way that was accurate, trustworthy, immediately understandable, and drill-down capable without overwhelming users.

We developed a custom visual language for data representation, allowing users to:

  • Compare portfolios and funds
  • Identify trends and anomalies quickly
  • Drill deeper into areas of interest on demand

Transaction Design & Security

Because large sums of money were being transferred — especially for institutional investors — there was zero tolerance for error.

We:

  • Mapped hundreds of transaction flows in detail
  • Designed multiple checks, validations, and confirmation states
  • Built fallback and recovery mechanisms for failure scenarios
  • Carefully coordinated UX with backend constraints and legacy systems

Security, clarity, and error prevention were treated as first-class design requirements.

Testing & Validation

We partnered with a professional usability testing firm to conduct extensive testing across all major user groups.

  • Developed detailed testing scripts and real-world scenarios
  • Iterated designs repeatedly based on findings
  • Validated both usability and decision confidence

Testing was continuous and directly informed refinements to IA, interaction patterns, and transaction flows.

Systems & Integration

On the backend, the platform had to integrate with:

  • Multiple legacy systems and databases (non-modifiable)
  • External data providers
  • Partner banks and financial institutions

UX design had to work within strict technical constraints while still delivering a modern, coherent experience.

Mobile Strategy

Insights gained from the main platform led us to propose and design multiple mobile applications, each optimized for specific use cases:

  • Advisor apps to support sales conversations and pitches
  • Investor apps for account monitoring, research, and transactions
  • Institutional tools for rapid response to market changes

Each app was purpose-built, rather than a simplified version of the desktop experience.

Design System & CMS

I designed and led the creation of a Component System Library — effectively a design system tightly integrated with a custom CMS built from scratch.

  • Design rules were encoded directly into the CMS
  • New pages and content automatically adhered to system standards
  • Ensured consistency, scalability, and governance across thousands of pages

This allowed Lord Abbett teams to scale content and features without breaking the experience.

Outcome
  • Successfully redesigned Lord Abbett's digital ecosystem end to end
  • Delivered personalized, role-based experiences for diverse user groups
  • Improved clarity and usability in highly complex, data-heavy workflows
  • Established a scalable UX and component foundation for future growth
  • Received strong positive feedback from users and client leadership

Beyond the product outcomes, the project provided deep insight into the operational, technical, and human realities of global finance at scale.
