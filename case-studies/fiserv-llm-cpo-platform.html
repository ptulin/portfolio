Fiserv — AI CFO Office — LLM-Powered Decision Platform

Principal Product Designer · UX Lead · LLM Interaction Design

Context

Fiserv is a global fintech leader providing payments, banking, and financial services technology to thousands of institutions worldwide. The organization operates at massive scale, handling mission-critical financial data, workflows, and regulatory requirements.

I worked on a strategic initiative to design and deliver an AI-powered CFO Office platform, leveraging large language models (LLMs) to help finance leaders generate reports, explore data, and make faster, more informed decisions across complex enterprise systems.

Challenge

Finance leaders at large institutions faced persistent problems:

  • Financial data fragmented across multiple systems
  • Reporting cycles that were slow, manual, and error-prone
  • Limited ability to explore data interactively or ask ad-hoc questions
  • High dependency on analysts and technical teams for insights
  • Growing pressure to adopt AI responsibly within regulated environments

The challenge was to design an AI system that could accelerate insight without sacrificing trust, accuracy, or governance.

My Role

Principal Product Designer / UX Lead

I was responsible for:

  • Experience strategy for the AI CFO Office platform
  • Interaction design for LLM-driven workflows
  • Leading a multidisciplinary design team
  • Aligning design with data, engineering, and compliance stakeholders
  • Translating complex financial logic into usable experiences
Approach

Research & Discovery

We conducted research with:

  • CFOs and finance leaders
  • Financial analysts and controllers
  • Internal stakeholders across data, compliance, and engineering

Key insights:

  • Users wanted speed, but only with confidence
  • AI needed to be explainable and auditable
  • Trust depended on visibility into sources, assumptions, and limits
  • Familiar financial mental models mattered more than novelty

LLM-Driven Interaction Design

Rather than building a generic chatbot, we designed purpose-built AI workflows:

  • Natural-language report generation
  • Context-aware prompts grounded in financial structure
  • Guided exploration of financial metrics
  • Controlled query patterns to reduce hallucinations and errors

LLMs were treated as co-pilots, not autonomous decision-makers.

Custom Workflows & Dashboards

The platform supported:

  • Role-based dashboards for different finance functions
  • Customizable workflows aligned to reporting cycles
  • Drill-downs from summaries to transactional detail
  • Saved views and repeatable reporting templates

This allowed finance teams to move fluidly between overview and detail.

Explainability & Trust

Given the regulatory environment, explainability was non-negotiable.

We designed:

  • Clear attribution of data sources
  • Visibility into assumptions and transformations
  • Structured outputs aligned to accounting standards
  • Guardrails that constrained AI behavior to approved data domains

These patterns increased confidence and adoption.

Enterprise Constraints

The platform had to operate within:

  • Strict data governance rules
  • Existing legacy systems
  • Complex permissions and access controls
  • Audit and compliance requirements

UX decisions were made with these constraints in mind from day one.

Outcomes

While specific metrics are confidential, outcomes included:

  • Faster report generation and analysis
  • Reduced reliance on manual analyst workflows
  • Increased confidence in AI-assisted decision-making
  • Strong internal alignment across product, data, and compliance teams
  • A scalable foundation for future AI-driven finance tools

The project demonstrated how LLMs can deliver real enterprise value when paired with disciplined UX and governance.
