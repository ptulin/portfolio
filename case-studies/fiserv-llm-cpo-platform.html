The Challenge

Small and mid-sized banks drowning in regulatory reporting.

CFO teams across small and mid-sized banks face a universal problem: too much reporting work, too few people, and no in-house data specialists. Many were outsourcing reporting at extremely high cost simply to stay compliant.

As Fiserv began centralizing financial data into Snowflake and exploring LLM capabilities, a transformative opportunity emerged.

Design a reporting workspace where CFOs could analyze, assemble, and generate regulatory outputs through a blend of conversation and direct manipulation — without needing data scientists.

Research Insights

Key findings that shaped the design direction.

  • Data identification was the biggest bottleneck

    CFOs spent most time finding data sources rather than analyzing them.

  • No internal analytics resources

    Small banks lacked data science expertise, forcing expensive outsourcing.

  • Outsourcing was expensive but unavoidable

    Many banks spending tens of thousands monthly on external services.

  • Workflows were consistent enough to automate

    Core reporting patterns similar across institutions.

  • CFOs needed familiar system behavior

    Solution had to feel intuitive without extensive training.

Design Strategy

A balanced approach to AI product design

Elements of AI Product Design

Traditional Visual Design & Wireframing — 10%

While foundational, these elements form only the basic outline of our product's aesthetic and functional framework.

Service Design Integration — 30%

Transition from classic service paradigms to those augmented by AI capabilities, translating conventional workflows into AI-enhanced processes.

Conversation Design — 30%

Defining conversational patterns is essential in establishing an engaging dialogue between our AI applications and users.

Interaction Design — 30%

This focuses on designing the interactive elements that will engage users, encouraging adoption and long-term adjustment to AI integration.

Iterative vs. Waterfall Approach

We adopted an iterative approach that adapts swiftly to evolving tech landscapes, invents and repurposes interactive patterns dynamically, enables data-driven feature design, ensures product quality through continuous iteration, and makes costs and schedules easily estimable and controllable.

Design System

Key design patterns and interaction models

Three-Panel Workspace

A three-panel design with automatic content strategy serves well in both traditional and AI design. It can be easily implemented in both systems and allows content reuse from the traditional version.

Three Panel Workspace — Main conversation panel, background information panel, and scrapbook panel for visualizations

Pattern for AI Version

  • Interaction in AI is focused on conversation in the Main Panel
  • Background Information provides context for ongoing conversation
  • Scrapbook Panel stores visualizations being discussed
  • Everything is resizable to customize experience

Bento Style Layout

Bento Style is well suited to AI-based interfaces and dashboards. It's naturally suited for card-based design, modern and considered a key design trend in 2024. Dark mode is preferred by data and financial users.

Bento Dashboard Layout — Card-based design optimized for financial data visualization

Interaction Patterns

How users navigate and interact with the system

Dashboard Interaction

  • Each card serves as a button
  • Content in all panels changes to focus on choices
  • Switches from dashboard to data analytics mode
  • Conversation about chosen visualization is triggered
  • Text-based conversation with supporting interaction elements

Analytics Interaction

  • Visualization becomes focal point of conversation
  • Related Data panel populated with background data
  • Visualization displayed in Scrapbook panel
  • Always in-view when conversation scrolls
  • User can interact via text or direct manipulation
  • Can return to dashboard at any point

Key Design Decisions

Critical choices that shaped the product

  • Conversation as primary navigation

    Making AI conversation the main way users interact, rather than traditional menus.

  • Persistent 3-panel context model

    Maintaining context across panels so users always understand where they are.

  • Direct manipulation via drag-and-drop

    Allowing users to directly manipulate visualizations and reports.

  • Scrapbook as working memory

    Providing dedicated space for visualizations being discussed.

  • Snowflake-grounded AI responses

    Ensuring all AI responses based on actual data, not hallucinations.

  • Built-in collaboration

    Enabling multiple users to work together with review workflows.

  • Bento dashboards for structured KPIs

    Using card-based layouts familiar to financial users.
