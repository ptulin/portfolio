The Challenge

Small and mid-sized banks were drowning in regulatory reporting

CFO teams across small and mid-sized banks face a universal problem: too much reporting work, too few people, and no in-house data specialists. Many were outsourcing reporting at extremely high cost simply to stay compliant.

As Fiserv began centralizing financial data into Snowflake and exploring LLM capabilities, a transformative opportunity emerged.

Design a reporting workspace where CFOs could analyze, assemble, and generate regulatory outputs through a blend of conversation and direct manipulation — without needing data scientists.

Research Insights

Key findings that shaped the design direction

  • Data identification was the biggest bottleneck — CFOs spent most of their time finding the right data sources rather than analyzing them
  • No internal analytics resources — Small banks lacked data science expertise, forcing expensive outsourcing
  • Outsourcing was expensive but unavoidable — Many banks were spending tens of thousands monthly on external reporting services
  • Workflows were consistent enough to automate — Despite variations, core reporting patterns were similar across institutions
  • CFOs needed familiar system behavior — Any solution had to feel intuitive and not require extensive training

Design Strategy & Approach

A balanced approach to AI product design

Elements of AI Product Design

Traditional Visual Design & Wireframing — 10%

While foundational, these elements form only the basic outline of our product's aesthetic and functional framework.

Service Design Integration — 30%

Transition from classic service paradigms to those augmented by AI capabilities, translating conventional workflows into AI-enhanced processes that deliver a more intuitive customer experience.

Conversation Design — 30%

Defining conversational patterns is essential in establishing an engaging dialogue between our AI applications and our users, making interactions as natural and efficient as possible.

Interaction Design — 30%

This focuses on designing the interactive elements that will engage our users, encouraging adoption and long-term adjustment to AI integration.

We adopted an iterative approach that:

Design System

Key design patterns and interaction models

Three-Panel Workspace

A three-panel design with automatic content strategy serves well in both traditional and AI design. It can be easily implemented in both systems and allows content reuse from the traditional version.

Three Panel Workspace — Main conversation panel, background information panel, and scrapbook panel for visualizations

Pattern for AI Version

  • Interaction in AI is focused on conversation which is performed in the Main Panel
  • Background Information is related to the topic of ongoing conversation to give it context
  • Scrapbook Panel is a temporary storage of visualizations being discussed in the conversation
  • Everything is resizable to customize experience

Scrapbook Panel — Working memory for visualizations during conversation

Bento Style Layout

Bento Style for design is well suited to AI-based interfaces as well as dashboards. Bento is naturally suited for card-based design, modern and considered one of the design trends in 2024. Dark mode is preferred by data and financial users.

Bento Dashboard Layout — Card-based design optimized for financial data visualization

Interaction Patterns

How users navigate and interact with the system

Dashboard Interaction Patterns

  • Each card can serve as a button
  • When pressed, content in all panels will change to focus on choices and we switch from dashboard mode to data analytics mode
  • Conversation about chosen visualization will be triggered
  • Conversation design will be text based with other interaction elements inserted to support best user experience

Drag and Drop Interaction

Report Composition Interface

Analytics Interaction Patterns

  • User pressed one of the data visualization cards
  • The same visualization becomes a focal point of conversation
  • Related Data panel is populated with background data
  • The visualization is also displayed in the Scrapbook panel. This way it is always in-view when the conversation scrolls
  • User can interact with the chart via text or interaction with the content
  • At any point user can return to the dashboard

System Architecture

Combining conversational AI with direct manipulation

The system combines conversational AI with direct manipulation and structured analytical workflows. It integrates with Snowflake for data access and uses LLM capabilities for natural language understanding and generation.

AI Workflow Architecture — Integration between conversational interface, data layer, and reporting engine

Key Design Decisions

Critical choices that shaped the product

  1. Conversation as primary navigation — Making AI conversation the main way users interact with the system, rather than traditional menus and forms
  2. Persistent 3-panel context model — Maintaining context across panels so users always understand where they are and what they're working with
  3. Direct manipulation via drag-and-drop — Allowing users to directly manipulate visualizations and reports, not just through conversation
  4. Scrapbook as working memory — Providing a dedicated space for visualizations being discussed, keeping them in view as conversation scrolls
  5. Snowflake-grounded AI responses — Ensuring all AI responses are based on actual data from Snowflake, not hallucinations
  6. Built-in collaboration — Enabling multiple users to work together on reports with review and approval workflows
  7. Bento dashboards for structured KPIs — Using card-based layouts that are familiar to financial users while supporting modern AI interactions

Impact & Outcomes

Measurable results from the design and implementation

The Data Compass platform has transformed how small and mid-sized banks handle regulatory reporting, delivering significant time and cost savings while improving accuracy and user satisfaction.

Reflection

Lessons learned and future directions

Designing AI for CFOs is about removing friction, not replacing expertise. This hybrid interaction model now serves as a foundation for future AI-powered reporting across Fiserv.
