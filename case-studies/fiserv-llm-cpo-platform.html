Fiserv — AI-Driven Data Compass Platform

Product Design · UX Design · UX Research

Overview

Fiserv operates at massive scale, serving financial institutions with complex regulatory, operational, and performance reporting needs. Executive teams and product leaders were overwhelmed by fragmented dashboards, slow reporting cycles, and limited ability to explore data conversationally.

I led product, UX design, and research for an AI-powered analytics workspace designed to consolidate signals from multiple Fiserv data sources into a single, executive-ready decision environment.

The goal was not "another dashboard," but a decision system: fast, trustworthy, and explainable.

Product Design

Problem

C-level executives and product leaders faced:

  • Multi-day reporting cycles
  • Disconnected tools across finance, risk, and operations
  • Heavy dependence on analysts for basic questions
  • Low confidence in data consistency across systems

Existing tools were optimized for analysts, not decision-makers.

Product Strategy

We reframed the product from reporting to data navigation.

Core principles:

  • Time-to-signal over data completeness
  • Progressive disclosure instead of dense dashboards
  • AI as an accelerator, not a black box
  • Trust and explainability as first-class requirements

This led to the concept of a Data Compass: a workspace that surfaces high-signal KPIs by default while allowing conversational drill-down across sources when needed.

Key Product Decisions

  • KPI-first executive home state
  • Conversational analytics layered on structured data
  • Proactive alerts for anomalies and risk signals
  • Clear separation between executive and analyst depth
UX Design

Design Challenge

Designing for executives meant optimizing for speed, clarity, and confidence — not exploration for its own sake.

UX Approach

We designed a single-page, modular workspace balancing overview and depth:

  • Primary KPI tiles for immediate situational awareness
  • Visual signal consolidation across disparate sources
  • Conversational entry points for natural-language questions
  • Contextual explanations to make AI outputs inspectable

The interface deliberately avoided dense tables, over-customization, and exposed system complexity.

Outcomes Observed

  • Executives answered common questions without analyst support
  • Reporting cycles shortened from days to minutes
  • Increased confidence due to consistent, cross-team signals
UX Research

Research Constraints

  • Highly regulated domain
  • Limited direct access to end users
  • Senior stakeholders with minimal time

Research Methods

  • Interviews with CFOs, CPOs, and business-line leaders
  • Workflow mapping of reporting and decision processes
  • Rapid concept validation with internal proxy users
  • Iterative design reviews with subject-matter experts

Key Insights

  • Executives value direction and risk over precision
  • Trust breaks when AI
