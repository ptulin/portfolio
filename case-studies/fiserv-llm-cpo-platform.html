The Story

Across small and mid-sized banks, CFO teams face a universal problem: too much reporting work, too few people, and no in-house data specialists. Many were outsourcing reporting at extremely high cost simply to stay compliant.

As Fiserv began centralizing financial data into Snowflake and exploring LLM capabilities, a transformative opportunity emerged:

Design a reporting workspace where CFOs could analyze, assemble, and generate regulatory outputs through a blend of conversation and direct manipulation — without needing data scientists.

What We Set Out to Build

  1. Automate the most painful parts of reporting.
  2. Unify dashboards, analytics, and AI conversation.
  3. Support real CFO workflows with minimal onboarding.
  4. Enable visual report composition.
  5. Guarantee accuracy and auditability.

Design Strategy & Approach

Elements of AI Product Design

Traditional Visual Design & Wireframing — 10%

While foundational, these elements form only the basic outline of our product's aesthetic and functional framework.

Service Design Integration — 30%

Transition from classic service paradigms to those augmented by AI capabilities, translating conventional workflows into AI-enhanced processes that deliver a more intuitive customer experience.

Conversation Design — 30%

Defining conversational patterns is essential in establishing an engaging dialogue between our AI applications and our users, making interactions as natural and efficient as possible.

Interaction Design — 30%

This focuses on designing the interactive elements that will engage our users, encouraging adoption and long-term adjustment to AI integration.

Iterative vs. Waterfall Approach

We adopted an iterative approach that:

  • Adapts swiftly to evolving tech landscapes
  • Invents and repurposes interactive patterns dynamically
  • Enables data-driven feature design & development
  • Ensures product quality through continuous iteration
  • Sustains ongoing innovation through constant design & R&D
  • Allows scheduled feature delivery & testing with client engagement
  • Makes costs and schedules easily estimable and controllable

Design Principles

  • Initiate design with available resources; evolve iteratively
  • Feature additions grounded in research
  • Experiment with styles; use data for refinements or pivots
  • Adopt continuous development with user data & feedback
  • Use similar design and interaction patterns as in the traditional version

Design Choices

Bento Style Layout

Bento Style for design is well suited to AI-based interfaces as well as dashboards:

  • Bento is naturally suited for card-based design
  • Bento is modern and considered one of the design trends in 2024
  • Dark mode is preferred by data and financial users

Design styles will be validated by user research and data

Bento Dashboard Layout

Three-Panel Design Pattern

A three-panel design with automatic content strategy can serve well in both traditional and AI design:

  • It can be easily implemented in both systems
  • We can also implement this design to content reused from traditional version of the website

Three Panel Workspace Overview

Pattern for AI Version

  • Interaction in AI is focused on conversation which is performed in the Main Panel
  • Background Information is related to the topic of ongoing conversation to give it a context
  • Scrapbook Panel is a temporary storage of visualizations being discussed in the conversation
  • Everything is resizable to customize experience

Scrapbook Panel - Working Memory

Interaction Patterns

Dashboard Interaction Patterns

  • Each card can serve as a button
  • When pressed, content in all panels will change to focus on choices and we switch from dashboard mode to data analytics mode
  • Conversation about chosen visualization will be triggered
  • Conversation design will be text based with other interaction elements inserted to support best user experience

Analytics Interaction Patterns

  • User pressed one of the data visualization cards
  • The same visualization becomes a focal point of conversation
  • Related Data panel is populated with background data
  • The visualization is also displayed in the Scrapbook panel. This way it is always in-view when the conversation scrolls
  • User can interact with the chart via text or interaction with the content
  • At any point user can return to the dashboard

Key Design Decisions

  1. Conversation as primary navigation.
  2. Persistent 3-panel context model.
  3. Direct manipulation via drag-and-drop.
  4. Scrapbook as working memory.
  5. Snowflake-grounded AI responses.
  6. Built-in collaboration.
  7. Bento dashboards for structured KPIs.
