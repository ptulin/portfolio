G2A — Global E-Commerce Platform Transformation

UX Strategy · UX Research · Product Design · Service Design

Overview

G2A is one of the world's largest digital marketplaces for games and digital goods, serving millions of users globally. When I joined, the platform had strong market traction but suffered from fragmented UX practices, limited research maturity, weak mobile experiences, and growing operational complexity across product, marketing, and engineering teams.

I was brought in to lead UX strategy and research, build a scalable UX organization, and modernize the platform across web, mobile, data, and emerging technologies.

Problem

G2A faced several interconnected challenges:

  • Fragmented user experience across web, mobile, and internal tools
  • No formal UX research or analytics-driven decision-making
  • Weak personalization and recommendation quality at scale
  • Declining mobile performance and conversion
  • Internal friction between product, engineering, and business teams
  • Loyalty mechanics that lacked long-term engagement incentives

At the same time, the platform was operating at massive scale, requiring solutions that worked reliably across countries, currencies, regulations, and user segments.

Context & Constraints
  • Global e-commerce platform with millions of users
  • High transaction volume and complex marketplace dynamics
  • Strong business pressure for growth, conversion, and retention
  • Distributed teams and rapidly evolving product roadmap
  • Need to balance speed, experimentation, and operational stability
Approach

I focused on raising UX maturity across the organization, not just redesigning screens.

Key pillars of the approach:

1. Build the UX organization

  • Scaled the UX team from 9 to 21 people
  • Hired designers, researchers, and data specialists
  • Introduced clear roles, rituals, pairing, and mentorship

2. Introduce research & analytics as decision drivers

  • Established continuous UX research practices
  • Implemented analytics-driven UX validation
  • Shifted product discussions from opinions to evidence

3. Modernize core user experiences

  • Redesigned major web and mobile flows
  • Improved checkout, discovery, and account management
  • Focused on reducing friction and cognitive load

4. Design for personalization and scale

  • Led UX strategy for AI-powered product recommendation systems
  • Defined interaction patterns for personalization without loss of trust
  • Balanced automation with user control

5. Innovate with loyalty and emerging tech

  • Designed blockchain-based loyalty and reward systems
  • Created new engagement mechanics tied to long-term retention
  • Ensured usability and transparency around complex concepts
Methods
  • UX research (qualitative + quantitative)
  • Funnel and behavioral analysis
  • Service design and journey mapping
  • Information architecture redesign
  • Rapid prototyping and experimentation
  • Cross-functional workshops
  • Design systems and component libraries
Key Contributions
  • Built and led a high-performing, multidisciplinary UX organization
  • Introduced analytics-driven UX decision-making across teams
  • Designed AI-powered product recommendation experiences
  • Led design of blockchain-based loyalty systems
  • Modernized mobile and web UX across key user journeys
  • Improved internal operations using service design principles
  • Established UX rituals, governance, and quality standards
Outcomes & Impact
  • Improved conversion and engagement across major funnels
  • Increased retention through personalized recommendations and loyalty mechanics
  • Stronger mobile performance and usability
  • Reduced internal friction and clearer cross-team alignment
  • Higher overall UX quality and consistency across products

G2A's UX transformation and leadership approach received external recognition, including being featured in Harvard Business Review (Poland Edition).

Role & Responsibility

UX Strategy & Research Lead

  • Owned UX strategy across multiple product lines
  • Led hiring, team growth, and organizational design
  • Partnered closely with product, engineering, marketing, and leadership
  • Balanced strategic vision with hands-on execution
  • Acted as internal advocate for user-centered, evidence-driven design
Why This Project Matters

This project represents a full UX maturity transformation at scale — combining leadership, research, AI-driven personalization, service design, and emerging technologies in a high-pressure, global e-commerce environment.

It shaped my approach to:

  • Building UX organizations
  • Designing AI-powered systems responsibly
  • Balancing innovation with operational reality
  • Leading design as a strategic business function
