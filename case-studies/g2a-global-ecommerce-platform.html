Pawel Tulin

G2A — Global E-Commerce Platform Transformation

UX Strategy · UX Research · Product Design · Service Design

Context

G2A is one of the world's largest digital marketplaces for games and digital goods, operating globally with millions of users, thousands of sellers, and extremely high transaction volume. The platform spans multiple regions, currencies, payment systems, and regulatory environments, creating significant UX, operational, and organizational complexity.

I joined G2A at a moment of rapid growth, where product velocity outpaced UX maturity. The company needed not only better interfaces, but a fundamental shift in how design, research, and data informed product decisions.

Challenge

Despite strong market traction, G2A faced systemic issues:

  • Fragmented UX across web, mobile, and internal tools
  • No formal UX research or analytics-driven decision-making
  • Weak personalization and discovery in a massive catalog
  • Declining mobile experience and conversion
  • Inconsistent design quality across teams and regions
  • Loyalty mechanics that failed to drive long-term retention
  • Design treated as execution, not strategy

The challenge was to raise UX maturity across the organization while operating at full production scale.

My Role

UX Strategy & Research Lead

I was responsible for:

  • Defining UX strategy across the platform
  • Building and leading the UX organization
  • Introducing research, analytics, and experimentation
  • Partnering with product, engineering, and leadership
  • Designing systems that scale globally

This was both a hands-on design role and a leadership role, with direct impact on product direction and organizational structure.

Organizational Transformation

One of my first priorities was building a sustainable UX function.

  • Scaled the UX team from 9 to 21 people
  • Hired designers, researchers, and data specialists
  • Introduced clear roles, ownership, and design standards
  • Established critique, review, and cross-team alignment rituals
  • Embedded UX into product and engineering workflows

This shift allowed design to move from reactive delivery to strategic partnership.

Research & Analytics as Decision Drivers

Prior to my involvement, UX decisions were largely opinion-driven.

I introduced:

  • Continuous qualitative research
  • Funnel and behavioral analytics
  • UX benchmarking and heuristic audits
  • Evidence-based design reviews

This changed internal conversations from "what do we think?" to "what does the data and user behavior show?"

Key Product Initiatives

AI Product Recommenders

To address discovery challenges in a massive catalog, I led UX strategy for AI-powered recommendation systems, using:

  • Behavioral signals
  • Category clustering
  • Purchase and browsing patterns
  • Contextual relevance

These systems were carefully designed to:

  • Improve discovery without overwhelming users
  • Increase conversion and average order value
  • Preserve trust and transparency

Blockchain Loyalty Systems

I led design efforts for blockchain-based loyalty and rewards mechanisms, focused on long-term retention rather than short-term promotions.

Key aspects included:

  • Token-based reward systems
  • Tiered engagement mechanics
  • Cross-device visibility and persistence
  • Integration with promotions and seasonal campaigns

The challenge was making advanced technology understandable and usable, not just innovative.

Service Design at Scale

G2A's experience extended far beyond the UI.

I mapped and redesigned service flows covering:

  • Onboarding and identity
  • Pricing, taxes, and regional differences
  • Refunds, disputes, and trust mechanisms
  • Customer support escalation paths

Service blueprints ensured consistency across systems, teams, and markets.

Team Building & Management

Beyond product outcomes, I focused on operational excellence:

  • Design onboarding frameworks
  • Shared component libraries and patterns
  • Clear documentation and governance
  • Alignment between UX, product, and engineering roadmaps

This reduced friction, increased delivery speed, and improved design quality at scale.

Outcomes & Impact

While internal metrics are confidential, outcomes included:

  • Improved conversion across key funnels
  • Increased engagement and retention via personalization and loyalty
  • Stronger mobile experience and usability
  • Clearer cross-team alignment and execution
  • A measurable increase in UX maturity across the organization

The work and organizational approach were recognized externally, including being featured in Harvard Business Review (Poland Edition).

What This Project Demonstrates
  • UX strategy at global marketplace scale
  • AI-powered personalization and discovery
  • Blockchain-based loyalty UX
  • Service design for complex systems
  • Design organization building and leadership
  • Analytics-driven decision-making
  • UX as a business and growth driver
