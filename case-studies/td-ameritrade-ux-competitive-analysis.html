Pawel Tulin

TD Ameritrade — UX Competitive Analysis & Heuristic Evaluation

Director of UX · UX Research · Heuristic Evaluation

Context

While serving as Director of UX at a design consultancy, I was asked to step in and support a short-term research engagement for TD Ameritrade — outside the scope of my primary project work.

The research team was short on resources, and the client needed a rapid but rigorous UX assessment of the TD Ameritrade web experience and trading platform interface. My role was to apply core UX research methodologies to identify usability issues and competitive opportunities that could inform future design work.

Challenge

TD Ameritrade's digital presence is broad and complex, covering:

  • Retail investor onboarding and education
  • Account management and funding
  • Market research tools
  • Real-time trading interfaces
  • Options and advanced trading workflows

This complexity posed two key research challenges:

  1. Usability gaps and inconsistencies across functional areas
  2. A fragmented experience relative to emerging digital competitors

Given limited time and resources, the deliverables needed to be focused, actionable, and rigorously structured.

My Role

As Director of UX, I deployed two complementary research methods:

Heuristic Evaluation

A systematic usability assessment using established UX principles to identify interface issues, interaction breakdowns, and cognitive friction points.

Competitive Analysis

A comparative study of key competitors (e.g., Schwab, E*TRADE, thinkorswim) to benchmark patterns, strengths, and opportunities across high-impact user flows.

I led both analyses from end to end, synthesizing findings and articulating insights in formal deliverables.

Heuristic Evaluation

The heuristic evaluation focused on:

  • Clarity of information hierarchy
  • Efficiency of key flows (e.g., login, account access, trade execution)
  • Feedback and error prevention
  • Consistency of UI patterns
  • Learnability and novice support

Findings included:

  • Redundant navigation elements causing cognitive overhead
  • Inconsistent labeling across similar tasks
  • Opportunities to streamline task flows for frequent users
  • UI density contributing to visual clutter

Recommendations were prioritized by impact and effort, with clear examples and references to known usability principles.

Competitive Analysis

The competitive analysis examined TD Ameritrade relative to key industry peers, evaluating:

  • Onboarding and account setup
  • Market research and educational tools
  • Trading interface layouts and controls
  • Mobile responsiveness
  • Trust signals and information transparency

Insights emerged around:

  • Best practices in onboarding that reduced user anxiety
  • Successful integration of educational assets into workflows
  • Competitive gaps in visual trade consoles and data presentation

These findings helped contextualize TD Ameritrade's strengths and identify targeted opportunities for improvement.

Deliverables

Heuristic Evaluation Report
Detailing UX violations, severity ratings, and recommended actions

Competitive Analysis Summary
Benchmarking key competitors and identifying strategic implications

Both reports were delivered to the product and design teams and used as reference tools for future design planning and prioritization.

Outcome & Impact

Although this was a short-term engagement, it provided:

  • Actionable UX insights grounded in established methodologies
  • A clear baseline for prioritizing improvements
  • Strategic context from competitor benchmarks
  • Validation of existing strengths and targeted opportunity areas

For my portfolio, this case highlights:

  • Strong research breadth (heuristics + competitive)
  • Ability to deliver high-quality outputs under time and resource constraints
  • Domain fluency in financial UX and complex interfaces
