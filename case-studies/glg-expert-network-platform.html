Gerson Lehrman Group — Client & Internal Platform Redesign

Information Architecture · Product Design · Compliance-by-Design UX

Context

Gerson Lehrman Group (GLG) is the world's leading expert network, operating at the intersection of finance, consulting, healthcare, government, and industry. The platform connects institutional clients with subject-matter experts globally, enabling time-sensitive, high-stakes decision-making.

Because GLG operates in heavily regulated environments (SEC, FINRA, confidentiality and non-public information handling), compliance, conflict avoidance, and information security are not features — they are core system requirements.

I worked as an Information Architect and Product Designer across both client-facing platforms and internal enterprise systems, modeling strict legal and compliance constraints directly into product interactions, workflows, and training experiences.

The Challenge

GLG's platform needed to support:

  • Millions of expert profiles across industries and geographies
  • High-risk, high-value expert–client engagements
  • Strict confidentiality and conflict-of-interest rules
  • Complex screening, matching, and approval workflows
  • Internal tooling used by globally distributed GLG employees
  • Real-time visibility and control for clients

The challenge was to design powerful systems that remain safe by default, even under speed, scale, and pressure.

Compliance as a First-Class UX Constraint

Expert Contracts & Training

Experts are required to:

  • Sign and annually renew contracts agreeing to confidentiality obligations
  • Decline engagements that could violate employer or legal restrictions
  • Avoid sharing confidential or non-public information
  • Terminate engagements that become non-compliant

I worked on modeling these obligations directly into digital interactions, including:

  • Interactive, sector-specific training modules
  • Annual compliance tutorials in multiple languages
  • Scenario-based exercises highlighting inadvertent disclosure risks

Compliance was embedded into onboarding, renewal flows, and engagement touchpoints — not handled as static documentation.

Conflict Screening & Risk Management

GLG uses:

  • Extensive expert profile questionnaires
  • A large historical database of screening responses
  • Real-time conflict detection during expert matching

I contributed to the design of:

  • Screening workflows that automatically disqualify unsuitable experts
  • Assisted decision paths for trained GLG professionals handling edge cases
  • Graceful exits allowing experts to withdraw from engagements early while preserving trust and fairness

These systems balanced automation with human judgment, especially in ambiguous or high-risk scenarios.

Blinding Models Embedded in Product Design

To minimize conflicts of interest and prevent targeted disclosures, GLG employs multiple blinding strategies — implemented through UX and system logic rather than labeled explicitly:

Single-Blind Matching

Clients see expert profiles; experts do not see full client or project sponsor details.

Double-Blind Matching

Neither party sees identifying details until screening and approvals are complete.

Triple-Blind / Full Anonymization

Applied in highly sensitive engagements, limiting visibility even internally during early vetting stages.

I worked on modeling these blinding methods in online interactions, ensuring:

  • The correct level of anonymity is enforced automatically
  • Client-defined rules are respected
  • Transitions between states are clear, auditable, and compliant
Client Protections & Transparency

Clients were given direct control via a real-time compliance dashboard, allowing them to:

  • Monitor all active engagements
  • Review expert credentials and disclosures
  • Define custom rules (e.g., pre-approvals, additional screening questions)
  • Control how and when data is shared

This reduced the risk of soliciting confidential information and increased institutional trust in the platform.

Employer Constraints

Many experts are employed by corporations, institutions, or government entities. I worked on flows that enforced:

  • Automatic blocking of experts from consulting on their employer's projects
  • Employer-specific restrictions stored in a centralized preferences database
  • Requirements for written consent for deeper or longer engagements

These constraints were invisible to users when irrelevant, and unavoidable when required.

Internal Search & Intelligence Platform

A major part of my work focused on internal GLG systems used by employees worldwide.

GLG had access to:

  • Hundreds of governmental, medical, industrial, and scientific databases
  • One of the largest non-governmental collections of expert, company, and industry data

I designed:

  • A highly advanced internal search engine
  • Deep filtering systems supporting complex queries across people, companies, and topics
  • Interactive data visualizations (graphs, relationship maps, timelines)

These tools allowed employees to:

  • Follow individuals, companies, or industries over time
  • Understand relationships, influence, and exposure
  • Make informed matching decisions quickly and safely

The search and visualization capabilities exceeded public search engines in depth and precision, while remaining usable under daily operational pressure.

Research & Collaboration

Because these tools were internal:

  • I had direct access to employees across roles and regions
  • Research was continuous and deeply collaborative
  • Users were highly motivated to improve the product they relied on daily

This resulted in:

  • Rapid feedback loops
  • High adoption
  • Strong internal ownership of outcomes
