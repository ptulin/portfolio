ADP — Global Customer Support Chatbots (IBM Watson)

UX Designer · Conversation Designer · UX Research

Context

ADP is one of the world's largest payroll and human capital management companies, serving millions of businesses and employees globally. The scale and complexity of ADP's operations generate an enormous volume of customer support interactions, spanning payroll, taxes, benefits, compliance, and HR administration.

I worked on the design and delivery of AI-powered customer support chatbots built on IBM Watson, focused on reducing support load, improving response times, and increasing satisfaction for both customers and support teams.

Challenge

ADP's support organization faced several critical challenges:

  • Extremely high volume of repetitive, time-sensitive customer inquiries
  • Long wait times and heavy reliance on human agents
  • Complex domain knowledge required to answer payroll and compliance questions
  • Inconsistent customer experience across channels
  • Limited scalability of traditional support models

The goal was to introduce automation without degrading trust, clarity, or service quality.

Scope Clarification

While employees used the system internally, the primary users were customers — businesses and individuals interacting with ADP's support ecosystem at massive scale.

Any solution had to:

  • Work 24/7
  • Handle ambiguity safely
  • Escalate to humans when needed
  • Be accurate in a legally sensitive domain
My Role

UX Designer / Conversation Designer

I was responsible for:

  • Research and analysis of customer support behavior
  • Conversation design and dialog modeling
  • Designing logic flows and disambiguation rules
  • Defining analytics and feedback loops
  • Designing fallback and escalation mechanisms

This was a deeply hands-on role in a highly constrained AI environment.

Research & Discovery

We conducted extensive research to understand real support needs:

  • Shadowed customer service representatives
  • Reviewed large volumes of support logs and call transcripts
  • Classified questions, intents, and utterances
  • Identified ambiguity patterns and failure points

This research formed the foundation of all conversation design decisions.

Conversation Design at Scale

Because this work predated modern prompt-based LLM systems, everything had to be explicitly designed and programmed:

  • Intents and entities were manually defined
  • Dialog trees and logic maps were carefully constructed
  • Disambiguation rules were crafted for edge cases
  • Error handling was explicitly designed, not inferred

I created large-scale conversation logic maps to ensure:

  • Consistency
  • Predictability
  • Safe handling of sensitive topics
Analytics & Continuous Improvement

To improve performance over time, we designed:

  • Usage tracking and intent success metrics
  • Drop-off and escalation analysis
  • Feedback loops tied to conversation outcomes

These insights allowed continuous refinement of both logic and content.

Human Fallback & Trust

Automation was never absolute.

We designed:

  • Seamless escalation paths to human agents
  • Context preservation during handoff
  • Clear signaling when a bot could not safely proceed

This protected both customers and support staff from frustration and risk.

Outcomes

Once deployed, the system delivered immediate and sustained impact:

  • ~90% of human support interactions automated
  • ~70% reduction in support costs
  • Faster response times and 24/7 availability
  • Higher customer satisfaction for routine inquiries
  • Reduced workload for support agents, allowing focus on complex cases
  • Rapid ROI — the project paid for itself in approximately one year

The solution was a success across all stakeholder groups: customers, support teams, and leadership.

What This Project Demonstrates
  • Conversation design before modern LLM tooling
  • AI UX in highly regulated, sensitive domains
  • Large-scale intent modeling and disambiguation
  • Research-driven automation design
  • Analytics-informed iteration
  • Designing trust and fallback into AI systems
