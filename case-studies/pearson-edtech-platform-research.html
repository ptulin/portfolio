Pawel Tulin

Pearson — Online Education Platform

Product Design · UX Research · National-Scale Education Platform

Context

Pearson, in collaboration with the U.S. Department of Education, led the development and nationwide rollout of the Common Core Standards, establishing a unified framework for K–12 education across the United States. As part of this initiative, Pearson partnered with Apple to deploy iPads to students and teachers across public schools.

The objective was to design and build a large-scale digital education platform supporting students, teachers, administrators, and system operators—serving millions of users concurrently while aligning tightly with Common Core requirements.

Challenge

This initiative was not a single product but a national-scale education ecosystem with extreme complexity:

Multiple primary user groups:

  • Students (across ages, abilities, and special needs)
  • Teachers
  • School administrators and principals
  • District and system administrators

Massive content requirements:

  • Digital lessons, exercises, exams, and assessments
  • Curriculum aligned to Common Core standards

Technical scale and performance constraints:

  • Millions of simultaneous users
  • Strict latency and responsiveness requirements

Continuous delivery:

  • Agile development with frequent releases
  • Real-time design iteration embedded in engineering teams

The platform needed to be pedagogically sound, highly usable, secure, and scalable at a national level.

Role

Initially hired as a Product Designer, working within a distributed design organization across multiple U.S. cities. Embedded directly within a large engineering team in New York City.

Responsibilities included:

  • Designing student, teacher, and administrator experiences
  • Leading and conducting user research and usability testing
  • Collaborating closely with product, engineering, and education experts
  • Rapid iteration and real-time design support within an agile environment
Research & Discovery

Research was extensive and foundational:

  • Hundreds of hours observing students and teachers in real classroom environments
  • Contextual inquiry in public schools across multiple regions
  • Design thinking workshops with educators and stakeholders
  • Collection and analysis of qualitative and quantitative research data

Key insights:

  • Younger students required playful, game-oriented experiences
  • Older students needed a more academic, structured interface
  • Teachers required fast, efficient tools for lesson creation, assessment, and monitoring
  • Administrators needed high-level dashboards to track performance, adoption, and outcomes
Design & Solution

Student Experience

  • Hundreds of interactive lessons, exercises, and exams
  • Personalized learning paths based on:
    • Grade level
    • Performance
    • Special educational needs
  • Age-appropriate design paradigms:
    • Playful, exploratory UI for younger students
    • More academic and focused UI for older students

Teacher Tools

  • Dashboards for:
    • Lesson creation and curriculum management
    • Assessment review and grading
    • Monitoring individual and class-level progress
  • Interfaces designed to reduce administrative burden and support instruction at scale

Administrator & Principal Dashboards

  • High-level views into:
    • Student performance trends
    • Curriculum coverage
    • Engagement and adoption metrics
  • Tools supporting data-driven decisions at school and district levels

Platform & System Considerations

  • Designed for millions of concurrent users
  • UX decisions accounted for:
    • Latency
    • System feedback under load
    • Response times at scale
  • Close collaboration with engineering to ensure technical feasibility
Testing & Validation

Once initial prototypes were developed, the team conducted in-depth usability testing:

  • Built a custom, state-of-the-art usability lab:
    • Cameras and one-way mirrors
    • Eye-tracking and screen-tracking technology
  • Tested with:
    • Students
    • Teachers
    • Administrators
  • Continuous iteration based on research findings
Delivery & Scale
  • Agile, continuous development methodology
  • Design embedded within engineering teams
  • Rapid iteration cycles and real-time design adjustments
  • Multi-year initiative with a multi-billion-dollar budget
  • One of the largest digital education platforms ever delivered
Impact
  • Enabled nationwide adoption of Common Core-aligned digital learning
  • Delivered personalized education at unprecedented scale
  • Improved teaching efficiency and visibility into student progress
  • Established a new benchmark for standards-based, large-scale educational platforms
What This Project Demonstrates
  • Designing and delivering at national scale
  • Deep, research-driven UX practice
  • Supporting radically different user groups within a single ecosystem
  • Operating effectively inside agile, high-pressure engineering environments
  • Translating educational policy and pedagogy into usable digital products
