Pawel Tulin

Pearson — Common Core Digital Education Platform

Product Designer · UX Research · National-Scale Education Platform

Context

Pearson, in partnership with the U.S. Department of Education, led the development of Common Core educational standards across the United States. As part of this initiative, Pearson also collaborated with Apple to deploy iPads at scale across public schools, creating the foundation for a nationwide digital education platform.

I worked as a Product Designer on one of the largest education technology initiatives ever undertaken, focused on designing software for students, teachers, administrators, and system operators, serving millions of users across the U.S. public education system.

Challenge

The project required designing a unified platform that could support:

  • Nationwide Common Core standards
  • Mass deployment across public schools
  • Vastly different user groups with distinct needs
  • Personalized education at scale
  • High system reliability and performance
  • Continuous iteration under active classroom use

This was not a single product, but an entire educational ecosystem.

Users

We designed for multiple, fundamentally different audiences:

Students

  • Younger children requiring playful, engaging interfaces
  • Older students needing more academic, structured experiences
  • Personalized lessons based on level and special needs

Teachers

  • Lesson planning and delivery tools
  • Assessment review and grading dashboards
  • Insight into individual and class-level progress

Administrators & Principals

  • School-level and district-level performance dashboards
  • Monitoring adoption, progress, and outcomes

System Administrators

  • Desktop tools for managing infrastructure, content, and access

Each group had unique goals, workflows, and success criteria.

Scope of Work

As part of a distributed design team across several U.S. cities, we designed:

  • Hundreds of interactive lessons and exercises
  • Digital tests and assessments
  • Teacher dashboards for monitoring and review
  • Administrative dashboards for performance oversight
  • System tools for platform management

Lessons were designed to be adaptive and personalized, responding to student performance and individual learning needs.

Research & Discovery

Research was a major pillar of the project:

  • Classroom observation with students and teachers
  • Design thinking workshops
  • Data collection and synthesis across schools
  • Iterative validation of content, interaction, and pedagogy

Insights from research informed both educational effectiveness and usability at scale.

Usability Testing at Scale

Once initial prototypes were ready, we conducted extensive usability testing:

  • Built a custom, high-end usability lab
  • Used cameras, one-way mirrors, eye tracking, and screen tracking
  • Tested with students, teachers, and administrators
  • Refined interaction models through repeated testing cycles

This allowed us to validate not only usability, but real classroom effectiveness.

Agile & Continuous Delivery

The platform was built using agile and continuous delivery methodologies.

I was embedded in a large development team in New York City, which required:

  • Rapid iteration and real-time design adjustments
  • Close collaboration with engineers and product owners
  • Immediate response to issues surfaced during classroom use

The system was designed to support millions of simultaneous users, making performance and latency key UX considerations.

Design Considerations at Scale

Key challenges included:

  • Designing for limited bandwidth and varying hardware conditions
  • Ensuring clarity and resilience in high-pressure classroom environments
  • Maintaining consistency across a massive content library
  • Balancing pedagogical goals with system constraints

UX decisions were deeply intertwined with technical realities.

Outcomes
  • A nationwide digital platform aligned with Common Core standards
  • Personalized learning experiences at unprecedented scale
  • Robust tools for teachers and administrators
  • High adoption across public schools
  • A scalable foundation for future digital education initiatives

The project represented a rare opportunity to shape how an entire country approaches digital education.
